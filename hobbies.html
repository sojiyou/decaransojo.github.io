My Hobbies!

volleyball

Volleyball wasn’t always my go-to sport. I used to hop between different activities like basketball, chess, and badminton. I initially thought volleyball was more of a girls' game, but that changed when I pulled off a great spike. It felt so satisfying that I kept asking for more tries, spiking again and again until the sun set. After that, I joined my high school volleyball team and trained hard, leading me to where I am today.

Coding

Coding is one of my hobbies because when I get tired of playing volleyball or mobile games, I dive into learning more advanced coding techniques. I’m confident in my skills with Java, Python, and C#. I find coding enjoyable, even when faced with challenges. I often laugh at small mistakes like missing a semicolon or forgetting to increment an index in a loop, which sometimes even causes my laptop to crash. Despite the challenges, I genuinely enjoy coding!

Music

Music is like a companion for me, especially when I’m by myself. I also listen to it while coding or playing mobile games. The type of music I choose depends on my mood—if I’m feeling excited or upbeat, I go for groovy tunes. When I want to relax, I turn to country or calming music. As I said, it all depends on how I’m feeling.

Anime

I wasn’t really into anime at first until a friend suggested it. Initially, I was hesitant and didn’t really want to watch, but my friend insisted and recommended "Black Clover." Since it was during the pandemic, I decided to give it a shot. I was biased during the first few episodes, thinking it wasn’t that great, but eventually, I started enjoying it more and more. By the time I finished the series, I was hooked and asked my friend for more anime recommendations.
